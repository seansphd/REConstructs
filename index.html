Re:Constructs

Improving Historical Algorithm Recreation Through Systems Thinking

Explore Research Full Paper (PDF)

Research

This study investigates whether systems thinking principles can improve the accuracy of large language models in recreating historical computer art algorithms from PAGE magazine, 1969 to 1974.

Systems Thinking

Decomposing algorithms into components, variables, interactions, and feedback loops to capture artistic intent.

AI Recreation

Using Claude 3.5 Sonnet to recreate historical computer artworks as interactive web applications.

Preservation

Balancing algorithmic accuracy with experiential authenticity in digital art preservation.

Methodology

Two distinct approaches tested to recreate nine historical computer artworks

Control Group

Direct input of original PAGE magazine descriptions to AI. Focus on technical precision.

Systems Group

Two stage process. Restructure with systems thinking, then recreate. Focus on conceptual relationships.

Framework

Components, inputs, feedback, and emergence are used as the organising schema.

Artworks

Compare Control and Systems approaches for each reconstructed artwork. Reports and screenshots are provided as links.

Results

Interactivity

7 of 9 showed improved interaction.

Clarity

7 of 9 achieved clearer visual outcomes.

Precision

4 of 9 reached stronger mathematical fidelity.
